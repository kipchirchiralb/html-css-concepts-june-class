CSS units - Relative units and Fixed units

Fixed units - px, in, cm ..

Fixed units are absolute measurements that do not change based on the context or viewport size. They include:

  • px - Pixels, the most common unit for web design.
  • in - Inches, typically used in print media.
  • cm - Centimeters, also used in print media.
  • mm - Millimeters, another unit for print media.
  • pt - Points, often used in typography (1 pt = 1/72 inch).
  • pc - Picas, where 1 pica = 12 points.

Relative units - em, rem, vw, vh ..

Relative units adjust based on the context, such as the size of the parent element or the viewport. They include:

  • em - Relative to the font size of the element (or its parent).
  • rem - Relative to the root element's font size, providing consistency across the document.
  • vw - Viewport width, 1vw is 1% of the viewport's width.
  • vh - Viewport height, 1vh is 1% of the viewport's height.
  • vmin - The smaller value between vw and vh.
  • vmax - The larger value between vw and vh.
  • % - Percentage, relative to the parent element's size.
